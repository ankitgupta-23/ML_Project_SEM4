--- Brain Tumor Detection Model ---


Brain tumors are one of the most serious diseases in medical science. Effectiveness and efficacy analysis has always been a primary concern of radiologists in the early stages of tumor development.

The biopsy procedure requires the neurosurgeon to drill a small hole into the skull from which the tissue is collected. There are risk factors associated with the biopsy test, including bleeding from the tumor and brain causing infection, stroke, coma, and even death. But the main concern with the stereotactic biopsy is that it is not 100% accurate which may result in a serious diagnostic error followed by wrong clinical management of the disease.

Studies in the recent literature have also reported that automatic computerized detection and diagnosis of the disease, based on medical image analysis, could be a good alternative as it would save radiologists time and also obtain a tested accuracy.

Popular machine learning algorithms for classification of are Artificial Neural Network, Convolutional Neural Network, k-Nearest Neighbour (kNN), Decision Tree, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Na√Øve Bayes and Random Field (RF). This Model is using Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for the detection and classification of the brain tumor.

The model will classify and predict if a patient has a brain tumor or not if given his MRI scan image.

Who should use the Model:

  • Only a radiologist or neurologist should use the system
  • Should have well experience in diagnosing brain disease

Assumptions And Limitation:

  • The Input should be valid MRI image of the brain.
  • The results for predicted output will be used with other factors to achieve a conclusion.
  • The predicted output should not be considered as final conclusion for diagnosis.
